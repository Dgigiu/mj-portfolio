## The problem

Most recipe apps push the same dishes everyone else cooks. MyFoodways flipped the model: start from what's in the fridge, then suggest recipes that use those ingredients before they go to waste.

## What I owned

End-to-end product design across iOS and Android. Onboarding flow, ingredient capture, recipe browsing, weekly planning, and the visual system. I also worked with a small content team on recipe taxonomy.

## Key decisions

- **No login required to try the app.** Friction kills curiosity. Account creation came later, only when there was something to save.
- **Ingredient capture in three taps.** Most people gave up if it took longer.
- **Recipes ranked by what you have, not by what we're promoting.** A small product principle that shaped a lot of the UI.

## Outcome

The app reached a healthy active-user base and a 4.6 average rating before pivoting. Several patterns from this work, especially the ingredient-first browsing model, ended up in later projects.
