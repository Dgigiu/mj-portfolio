## The problem

Confluence is where many enterprise teams write documentation, but file management inside it has always been a weak point. Files attached to pages get lost, permissions are inherited in confusing ways, and there's no good way to see what your team actually has. Team Files set out to fix that.

## The team

A small founding team. I was the only designer, working alongside two engineers and a product-minded co-founder. We sold direct through the Atlassian Marketplace, so onboarding had to work without a sales call.

## What I owned

Everything visual and interactive: information architecture, product UI, the marketing site, the listing assets, support content. The design system started as a few CSS variables and grew to cover hundreds of screens.

## Highlights

Three things mattered most:

1. **A clear mental model.** Files belong to teams, not pages. Once that was right, the rest followed.
2. **Permissions that explain themselves.** We invested in a permissions UI that showed *why* someone could or couldn't see a file, not just the boolean answer.
3. **A native feel inside Atlassian's product.** We followed their patterns where it helped and broke them where it didn't.

## Outcome

Team Files grew to thousands of customers, including several Fortune 500 companies. The product is still running today.
